There is a crisis of violent, discriminatory police abuse targeting Black and brown communities and President Obama must be held accountable. Last week, the Department of Justice (DOJ) failed to hold Ferguson Officer Wilson accountable for tragically killing 18-year-old Michael Brown. And while the DOJ found Ferguson law enforcement guilty of widespread racism, it's not enough. Ferguson is America.

In the past 4 weeks, at least 4 Black and brown people have been killed or harmed by police:

  • 2/10/15 — Antonio Zambrano-Montes, 35-years-old, fatally shot 12 times by Pasco police with his hands in the air.
  • 3/1/15 — Africa, a man living on Skid Row in Los Angeles was fatally shot at least 5 times by Los Angeles police
  • 3/5/15 — Sergio Navas, unarmed, was fatally shot by Los Angeles police
  • 3/6/15 – A police officer in Madison, Wisconsin, shot and killed 19 year old Tony Robinson, who was unarmed.

Right now, President Obama is deciding what recommendations from the Task Force on 21st Century Policing will become law. But the reforms now being discussed, while important, will barely scratch the surface of a problem that demands deep, far-reaching transformation.
