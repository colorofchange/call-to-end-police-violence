Ferguson is America. There is a crisis of violent, discriminatory police abuse targeting Black and brown communities and President Obama must be held accountable. Recently, the Department of Justice (DOJ) failed to hold Ferguson Officer Darren Wilson accountable for tragically killing 18-year-old Michael Brown. And while the DOJ found the Ferguson Police Department guilty of widespread racism, it's not enough.

In the past 4 weeks, at least 4 Black or brown people have been killed or harmed by police:

  • 2/10/15 — Antonio Zambrano-Montes, 35-years-old, fatally shot 12 times by Pasco police with his hands in the air
  • 3/1/15 — Africa, a man living on Skid Row in Los Angeles was fatally shot at least 5 times by Los Angeles police
  • 3/5/15 — Sergio Navas, unarmed, was fatally shot by Los Angeles police
  • 3/6/15 – A police officer in Madison, Wisconsin, shot and killed 19 year old, unarmed Tony Robinson

Right now, President Obama is deciding which of the recommendations from the Task Force on 21st Century Policing will become law. The latest recommendations include important reforms to increase police oversight and accountability, but don't go far enough to end police militarization or create a clear pathway of enforcement. We need implementation of the reforms and even greater executive action. Will you call your representative today and urge them to publicly support a comprehensive executive order to enforce and expand the federal ban on discriminatory policing and strengthen police accountability nationwide?
